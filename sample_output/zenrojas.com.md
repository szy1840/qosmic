# Zen Rojas audit — a calm brand pointing demand at products it can't sell

## Executive summary

**Zen Rojas has a clear, premium identity and a benefit-named catalog — and then sends its best traffic to sold-out shelves.** The brand is tightly positioned around calm and daily ritual ("Discover your ritual," "Find your moment of Zen," veteran-owned, 100% organic), and its teas are named by the job they do: Unwind, Sleep, Heartburn, Bodyguard (`artifacts/zenrojas.com/pages/home-page.txt`, `artifacts/zenrojas.com/pages/collection-collections-teas.txt`). But the teas collection shows Premium Sencha, Heartburn, Sleep, and even the flagship **Unwind Organic Tea all marked "Sold Out"** (`artifacts/zenrojas.com/pages/collection-collections-teas.txt`), and the Unwind PDP itself renders a dead, greyed-out **"SOLD OUT" button with no back-in-stock capture** (`artifacts/zenrojas.com/screenshots/product-products-unwind.png`). The store spends its attention on products a shopper cannot buy and collects nothing when they bounce.

**Trust and proof are thin at exactly the moment of decision.** The Unwind PDP shows "No reviews yet" with no testimonials or ratings anywhere on the page (`artifacts/zenrojas.com/screenshots/product-products-unwind.png`), so a first-time visitor to a small unknown brand has no social proof to lean on. Worse, the one on-page revenue lever — a "Complete Your Ritual" cross-sell of four products — renders with **blank, broken product images** (`artifacts/zenrojas.com/screenshots/product-products-unwind.png`), so the merchandising that should raise order value instead signals a broken store.

**The growth foundations exist but are inert.** There is a $50 free-shipping threshold sitting above an $8–$13 price list, an Ambassador (referral) program, sampler SKUs from $2, and a genuine veteran/family story (`artifacts/zenrojas.com/pages/home-page.txt`, `artifacts/zenrojas.com/pages/content-pages-aboutus.txt`) — none of it wired into bundles, subscriptions, back-in-stock, or benefit-led routing. Technically the store is fast but ships **no structured data on any page** and **images with no dimensions or next-gen formats** (`artifacts/zenrojas.com/tech_signals.json`). The first tests are obvious: capture demand on sold-out PDPs and add proof; then convert the ritual story into bundles, subscriptions, and benefit-led landing.

## Proposed experiments

### exp-7c2a9e0f4b18 — Capture demand on sold-out product pages

**Pillar:** Conversion
**Affected surface:** Sold-out PDPs (Unwind, Sleep, Heartburn, Sencha)
**URL:** https://zenrojas.com/products/unwind
**Evidence:** `artifacts/zenrojas.com/screenshots/product-products-unwind.png` (dead "SOLD OUT" button), `artifacts/zenrojas.com/pages/collection-collections-teas.txt` (Unwind, Sleep, Heartburn, Sencha all "Sold Out")
**Hypothesis:** The homepage and catalog feature products that are out of stock, and the PDP offers only an inert "SOLD OUT" button — every high-intent visitor to these pages leaves with no way to be recovered. A back-in-stock email/SMS capture converts dead inventory traffic into a re-engageable list and recovered sales when stock returns.
**Primary change:** Replace the dead button on out-of-stock PDPs with a "Notify me when back in stock" email/SMS capture, and trigger a restock notification flow.
**Primary KPI:** Recovered conversions from back-in-stock notifications
**Decision rule:** Ship if back-in-stock signup rate >5% of OOS PDP sessions and restock emails convert above baseline email.
**Expected lift:** +10–20% (of otherwise-lost OOS sessions)
**Confidence:** 84%

### exp-2f8d1b6c3a90 — Add reviews and social proof to PDPs

**Pillar:** Conversion
**Affected surface:** All PDPs
**URL:** https://zenrojas.com/products/unwind
**Evidence:** `artifacts/zenrojas.com/screenshots/product-products-unwind.png` ("No reviews yet"; no ratings or testimonials on page)
**Hypothesis:** As a small, unfamiliar brand, Zen Rojas gives first-time shoppers no social proof at the decision point ("No reviews yet"), suppressing conversion. Seeding and surfacing reviews/ratings should raise PDP→cart rate.
**Primary change:** Stand up a reviews widget with star ratings near the buy box, seed it via a post-purchase review request and the Ambassador program, and show aggregate ratings on PDP + collection cards.
**Primary KPI:** PDP→add-to-cart rate
**Decision rule:** Ship if PDP→cart improves without increasing returns/complaints.
**Expected lift:** +6–12%
**Confidence:** 73%

### exp-9a4e7d2c1f63 — Free-shipping progress nudge toward $50

**Pillar:** AOV
**Affected surface:** Cart drawer + PDP buy box
**URL:** https://zenrojas.com/cart
**Evidence:** `artifacts/zenrojas.com/pages/home-page.txt` ("FREE SHIPPING ON ALL $50+ ORDERS"), `artifacts/zenrojas.com/pages/cart-cart.txt` (cart subtotal, taxes/shipping at checkout)
**Hypothesis:** The $50 free-shipping threshold sits well above the $8–$13 unit prices, so most carts fall short and shoppers don't know how close they are. A live progress bar ("You're $14 away from free shipping") plus a suggested add nudges multi-item orders.
**Primary change:** Add a free-shipping progress bar to the cart drawer and PDP with a one-tap recommended add-on (sampler or mug) to cross the threshold.
**Primary KPI:** Average order value
**Decision rule:** Ship if AOV rises without reducing checkout conversion rate.
**Expected lift:** +6–12%
**Confidence:** 72%

### exp-5a8d2c4e9f17 — Set image dimensions and serve next-gen formats

**Pillar:** Performance
**Affected surface:** All templates

**Evidence:** `artifacts/zenrojas.com/tech_signals.json` (every captured page: all images missing width/height, 0 WebP/AVIF)
