# Ginger People audit — overwhelming proof, a broken buy path

> Generated by the Qosmic runtime harness. Calibration target. Evidence is from
> an Internet Archive snapshot because the live edge (Cloudflare) blocked
> automated access — see provenance footer.

## Executive summary

**Ginger People has manufactured demand the way few specialty brands ever do — and then drops it at the buy step.** The hero PDP for GIN GINS® Original Ginger Chews carries 86 reviews, "Made with 10% fresh ginger – more than any other brand," "America's #1 selling ginger candy," and a 2024 Fiery Foods "Best Candy" award (`artifacts/gingerpeople.com/pages/product-products-gin-gins-original-ginger-chews.txt`). Yet the captured product page shows no price, no add-to-cart, no retailer module — the only purchase guidance is the sentence "Buy online or find it in the candy aisle at your local grocery or specialty store" (`artifacts/gingerpeople.com/screenshots/product-products-gin-gins-original-ginger-chews.png`). The page builds maximum intent and then asks the shopper to figure out the next step alone. For a brand this proof-rich, ambiguity at the PDP is the single largest leak.

**The adjacent purchase surface is not just weak — it is broken.** The `/cart` URL renders the literal, unprocessed WooCommerce shortcode `[woocommerce_cart]` as page text instead of a cart (`artifacts/gingerpeople.com/pages/cart-cart.txt`, `artifacts/gingerpeople.com/screenshots/cart-cart.png`). Any returning shopper, saved link, or "view cart" click lands on a page that displays template code. Combined with a legacy `/about_ginger_people.html` URL that 404s (`artifacts/gingerpeople.com/manifest.json`), the storefront leaks high-intent sessions at exactly the moments they signal readiness to buy.

**The content moat is deep and almost entirely uncommercialized.** The homepage is a wall of life-changing testimonials — pregnancy nausea, post-injury vertigo, motion sickness, morning sickness (`artifacts/gingerpeople.com/pages/home-page.txt`) — and the health blog runs substantive education like "6 Surprising Health Benefits of Turmeric" (`artifacts/gingerpeople.com/pages/content-6-surprising-health-benefits-of-turmeric.txt`), backed by a 40-year, award-winning heritage story (`artifacts/gingerpeople.com/pages/content-our-story.txt`). None of it routes to a product, a routine, or a reorder. The first tests should be structural: put a real buying box on every PDP and fix the cart; then turn the symptom testimonials and health content into guided shopping and reorderable routines.

## Proposed experiments

### exp-a1c7f0e94b22 — Add a buying box to every product page

**Pillar:** Conversion
**Affected surface:** GIN GINS Original PDP (pattern applies to all PDPs)
**URL:** https://gingerpeople.com/products/gin-gins-original-ginger-chews/
**Evidence:** `artifacts/gingerpeople.com/screenshots/product-products-gin-gins-original-ginger-chews.png`
**Hypothesis:** The PDP creates strong intent through proof (86 reviews, "America's #1 selling ginger candy," 10% fresh ginger, award) but the captured page shows no price, add-to-cart, or retailer module — only the sentence "Buy online or find it in the candy aisle." Converting that ambiguity into an explicit choice should lift purchase actions without changing traffic.
**Primary change:** Add a persistent "Choose how to buy" box directly under the product title: "Buy online," "Find near me," and compact retailer logos (Amazon/Thrive/grocery), with price and quantity where the store transacts directly.
**Primary KPI:** Outbound retailer + add-to-cart click rate
**Decision rule:** Ship if combined buy-action click rate improves without hurting PDP bounce or time-on-page.
**Expected lift:** +12–20%
**Confidence:** 80%

### exp-3f8b2d6a1e07 — Fix the broken cart page

**Pillar:** Conversion
**Affected surface:** `/cart`
**URL:** https://gingerpeople.com/cart
**Evidence:** `artifacts/gingerpeople.com/pages/cart-cart.txt` (renders `[woocommerce_cart]`), `artifacts/gingerpeople.com/screenshots/cart-cart.png`
**Hypothesis:** The cart URL renders the raw, unprocessed shortcode `[woocommerce_cart]` as visible page text instead of a functioning cart. Every returning shopper, saved link, partner referral, or "view cart" click that lands here sees template code and abandons. Restoring a working cart (or a branded recovery page if the store routes to retailers) recovers high-intent sessions.
**Primary change:** Render a real cart at `/cart`; if the brand is retailer-routed, replace it with a purchase-recovery page ("Continue shopping," "Find a store," "Shop online retailers," support).
**Primary KPI:** `/cart` exit rate
**Decision rule:** Ship if `/cart` exit rate drops without hurting site-wide conversion.
**Expected lift:** +10–18%
**Confidence:** 84%

### exp-4f6c8b3a1d72 — Serve next-gen, dimensioned product images

**Pillar:** Performance
**Affected surface:** PDP and homepage image templates
**URL:** https://gingerpeople.com/products/gin-gins-original-ginger-chews/
**Evidence:** `artifacts/gingerpeople.com/tech_signals.json` (images: 0 next-gen formats across all surfaces; GIN GINS PDP has 25 images, 7 with no width/height; Ginger Soother PDP 4 missing dims)
**Hypothesis:** Product templates ship only legacy raster formats (no WebP/AVIF) and many images omit width/height, slowing LCP and risking layout shift on image-heavy PDPs. Optimizing images should improve mobile load and Core Web Vitals, lifting conversion on slow connections.
**Primary change:** Convert product/hero images to WebP/AVIF with responsive `srcset`, set explicit dimensions, and lazy-load below the fold.
**Primary KPI:** Mobile LCP (and PDP conversion on mobile)
**Decision rule:** Ship if mobile LCP improves materially without visual regressions.
**Expected lift:** +4–9%
**Confidence:** 66%
